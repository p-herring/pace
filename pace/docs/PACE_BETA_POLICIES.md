# Pace beta: community, safety and participation policy

## The premise

Pace helps adults meet for running, cycling and swimming in public places. Every member agrees to make conservative, considerate decisions for themselves and others. Pace is a connector, not an organiser, coach, emergency service or insurer.

## Beta admission and identity

- Pace beta is 18+ only.
- An account requires a verified email, accepted terms, completed safety acknowledgement and a completed profile. Phone verification is not part of this closed beta; Pace may introduce it before a wider launch.
- One person may hold one account. Impersonation, false identity and sharing accounts are prohibited.
- Pace may pause, remove or review any account or plan to protect the community.

## Plan and meeting-place rules

- Anyone may host a plan once eligible to host.
- Plans must state sport, date/time, approximate distance, pace metric, meeting place, capacity and relevant conditions.
- The meeting place must be a public, lawful and reasonably safe location: trailhead, park, pool, beach access, cafe, public car park or similar.
- **Never use a home address, private residence or accommodation as a meeting place.** Do not ask participants to meet at one.
- Public plans show only the suburb/approximate area. Exact meeting instructions are disclosed only to confirmed participants.
- Hosts must not create discriminatory, unsafe, commercial, illegal or sexually targeted plans.
- Open-water plans must include conditions, a safe entry/exit plan and a weather/currents check. Each swimmer remains responsible for assessing conditions and their own ability.
- Cycling plans must clearly state road/gravel/MTB and no-drop, regrouping or drop policy. Hosts cannot imply professional traffic control or route safety.

## Visibility, joining and waitlists

- Hosts choose public, public within a chosen radius, group-members-only or private/invite-only visibility.
- Public and radius plans use instant join unless a host elects approval. Member/private plans may require approval.
- A plan capacity includes confirmed participants, not the host. When capacity is reached, new eligible joins enter a FIFO waitlist.
- The first person on the waitlist is automatically promoted when a confirmed person leaves. Pace records the queue order; hosts may not skip or reorder it except for a safety or policy reason recorded by Pace.
- Plans beginning within 10 minutes cannot accept new participants through the app.

## Cancellations, attendance and no-shows

- Participants should leave as soon as they know they cannot attend. A cancellation within two hours of the start is marked late, but is never a reason to punish someone for illness, safety or an emergency.
- Hosts must cancel in Pace if the plan cannot happen, especially for weather, water, route, traffic or safety concerns. They should provide as much notice as practical.
- Hosts may remove someone only for a specific safety, conduct or capacity reason; the affected member receives a notice and can report the decision.
- After a plan, hosts and participants can record attendance. Repeated no-shows or repeated late cancellations may reduce hosting/joining privileges after human review—never through an opaque automatic ban.

## Conduct, messaging and consent

- Be respectful. No harassment, hate, threats, unwanted sexual attention, intimidation, discrimination, spam or commercial solicitation.
- Keep plan chat relevant. Do not pressure anyone for contact details, transport, alcohol, money or a different meeting location.
- Consent matters: do not photograph, record, track or publish another member without permission.
- Use the in-app block and report controls. Blocking immediately hides future discovery and messaging between the two members.

## Safety at the activity

- Pace does not replace local emergency services. In an emergency, call local emergency services first.
- Members are responsible for their own equipment, health, insurance, hydration, clothing, navigation, traffic/water awareness and decision to participate.
- Do not attend if unwell, impaired, or if conditions do not feel safe. Anyone may leave a plan at any time.
- Report serious incidents promptly. Pace will preserve relevant account/plan information and may suspend involved accounts while reviewing.

## Moderation and enforcement

- Reports are reviewed by a human beta administrator. Immediate temporary restrictions may be applied where there is a credible safety risk.
- Outcomes can include guidance, a warning, plan removal, limited joining/hosting, temporary suspension or permanent removal.
- Pace keeps a minimal audit trail for safety and moderation decisions. Members may appeal an outcome by contacting beta support.
